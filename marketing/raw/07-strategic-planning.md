# Strategic Planning & Implementation

## 84. Three‑Month Plan – Set Goals and Commitments Over Three Months
The plan outlines specific outreach, prayer, study, and leadership tasks for the next quarter, providing a clear roadmap. By detailing concrete objectives, participants can track progress and adjust strategies as needed. The schedule also incorporates measurable milestones to celebrate achievements along the way. This clarity encourages disciplined time management and focused effort toward discipleship goals. The plan serves as a shared vision that aligns the group's actions with the broader mission of the church. Ultimately, it empowers believers to commit intentionally and pursue growth over a defined period.

## 85. Three‑Month Plan – Identify People to Share Testimony With
The plan lists specific individuals from the List of 100 to hear your testimony, making outreach intentional and measurable. By naming targets, participants can tailor their messages to address the unique needs or interests of each person. This approach ensures that evangelistic efforts are focused rather than scattered, increasing the likelihood of impact. The plan also encourages accountability by tracking which testimonies have been delivered and their outcomes. By preparing in advance, believers can practice clarity and authenticity in sharing their story. Ultimately, this exercise transforms personal testimony into a strategic tool for spreading the gospel.

## 86. Three‑Month Plan – Identify Accountability Group Partners
The plan names partners for weekly accountability, ensuring that commitments are upheld and spiritual growth is sustained. By pairing members, the group creates a network of support where progress can be monitored and obstacles addressed. The plan also outlines specific check‑in methods, such as prayer requests or progress reports. This structure promotes consistent accountability and encourages mutual encouragement. By identifying partners, believers reduce isolation and increase responsibility toward their discipleship journey. Ultimately, the plan fosters a culture of shared growth and perseverance.

## 87. Three‑Month Plan – Identify 3/3 Group Participants
The plan identifies who will join a 3/3 meeting, facilitating structured discipleship and spiritual growth within a small group. By selecting participants, the group ensures balanced representation of strengths and needs. The plan also schedules meeting times and outlines agenda items to maintain focus. This preparation allows each member to prepare spiritually and mentally for meaningful engagement. By committing to the group, participants reinforce accountability to one another. Ultimately, this plan creates a stable foundation for discipleship within the 3/3 structure.

## 88. Three‑Month Plan – Identify Prayer Walking Partners
The plan lists those who will walk and pray together, building a habit of public listening and intercession. By pairing partners, believers can support one another in maintaining consistency during walks. The plan also includes guidelines for prayer focus and safe walking routes, ensuring a purposeful experience. This partnership fosters accountability, encouraging each participant to remain committed to their shared goal. By walking together, members deepen their relationship with God and each other through shared physical activity. Ultimately, this plan promotes disciplined prayer life and mutual encouragement.
